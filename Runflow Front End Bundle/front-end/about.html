Core Concepts

RunFlow estimates crowd density and flow rate on each course segment and summarizes them as an operational Level of Service (LOS A–F). The goal: keep runners moving, keep people safe, and surface hotspots early so teams can act.

  • Density (runners/m²): how tightly packed runners are within a segment.
  • Flow rate (p/min/m): runners per minute per metre of width.
  • LOS (A–F): A/B comfortable, C constrained, D/E crowded/unstable, F breakdown.

Operational Use

  • Race Director: monitor “Worst LOS,” hotspot list; coordinate holds/wave adjustments if LOS escalates.
  • Course Marshal: if LOS D/E, clear obstructions, meter entries, open planned alternate shoulder.
  • Health & Safety: high density + dropping flow → compression risk; stage medical, water, short holds if needed.
